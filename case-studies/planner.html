Inquisitive Planner

Overview

The Inquisitive Planner is a digital tool designed to help primary school teachers seamlessly organise their curriculum, plan lessons, and track student progress in an intuitive and structured way. Unlike traditional planning tools, the Planner integrates directly with curriculum-aligned content, enabling teachers to structure their academic year efficiently.

One of the biggest challenges teachers face is the need to balance structured curriculum requirements with the flexibility to adapt lesson plans to their students’ unique needs. The Inquisitive Planner was built to bridge this gap by providing a system that allows for structured planning while remaining adaptable to real-time teaching dynamics.

Problem Statement

Primary school teachers are responsible for planning engaging, educational experiences while also ensuring compliance with national and regional curriculum standards. However, many teachers still rely on disjointed tools such as spreadsheets, paper planners, and static documents, which are difficult to update and lack real-time collaboration features.

The goal of the Inquisitive Planner was to create a user-friendly, intuitive, and efficient tool that enables teachers to structure their lesson planning while maintaining the flexibility to modify schedules based on student progress and classroom needs.

Research and Discovery

User Interviews

To ensure the Planner was designed with real teacher workflows in mind, we conducted in-depth interviews with 15 primary school educators from various backgrounds. These conversations helped us identify pain points, understand existing workarounds, and define the core functionalities the Planner needed to have.

Key Findings

  • Teachers need a way to quickly organise and adapt plans without starting from scratch.
  • Integration with existing content and curriculum standards is essential for seamless adoption.
  • Collaboration and sharing features would significantly enhance usability and efficiency.
  • Progress tracking was a key concern, as teachers wanted a clear way to monitor completion across lessons.
  • Providing a way to annotate lessons and units would enable teachers to keep personalised notes on their lesson plans.

Design Process

Wireframing & Prototyping

Based on our research findings, we began designing the first iteration of the Inquisitive Planner through low-fidelity wireframes in Figma. These wireframes outlined the basic navigation, the layout of the Planner’s different sections, and how teachers could interact with units, lessons, and curriculum structures.

After internal feedback and refinement, we moved on to high-fidelity prototypes, adding interactions to test how teachers could intuitively navigate between terms, add units, and track progress.

Usability Testing

To validate our design, we conducted usability testing with primary school teachers in real classroom scenarios. Teachers were asked to complete a set of tasks using the Planner, and we observed their interactions, noting areas where they encountered friction.

  • Iterative testing led to improved navigation and more intuitive workflows.
  • Teachers highlighted the importance of clear instructional cues and a seamless experience for modifying their plans.
  • We introduced a progress tracking system based on feedback that allowed teachers to mark lessons as complete.

Final Solution

The final iteration of the Planner includes the following core features:

  • Drag-and-drop lesson organisation across terms.
  • Preloaded curriculum-aligned templates that streamline lesson planning.
  • Unit progress tracking that updates dynamically as lessons are marked complete.
  • Annotations feature for teachers to jot down notes and reflections on lessons.
  • Easy sharing with co-teachers and administrators, fostering collaboration.

Impact & Results

Following the launch of the Planner, we conducted an analysis to measure its impact:

  • 80% of teachers reported saving at least 2 hours per week on planning.
  • Adoption rates exceeded initial projections by 35%, indicating strong demand.
  • Teachers praised the tool for its simplicity, curriculum alignment, and ease of use.
  • Annotations and progress tracking were identified as the most valuable features for daily teaching.

Next Steps

Looking forward, we plan to enhance the Planner with additional features based on teacher feedback, including:

  • AI-powered recommendations for lesson sequencing and differentiation.
  • Enhanced reporting features for better tracking of student progress.
  • Deeper integration with student assignment tracking to provide a holistic teaching and learning experience.

Conclusion

The Inquisitive Planner has revolutionised how primary school teachers structure their curriculum and manage their lessons. By integrating lesson planning, progress tracking, and collaboration in a single platform, it significantly reduces administrative workload and allows teachers to focus more on effective instruction.

With continuous enhancements on the horizon, the Planner is poised to become an indispensable tool for educators, ensuring a seamless and efficient teaching experience.
